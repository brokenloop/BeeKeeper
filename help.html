Donate

If you're interested in contributing to our project, consider calling us on 1800 833 833 and making a donation. Below you can see what your contribution can achieve.

Donation What it can achieve
€10 A monthly donation of €10 helps pay the costs of running BeeKeeper. This includes running our website, helping us organise awareness campaigns, and coordinating petitions against the use of pesticides. These donations are the backbone of our organisation, and allow us to keep looking out for the little guys.
€50 For €50 we can provide materials to existing BeeKeeper sponsored apiaries. These apiaries are at the center of our scientific research into the causes of CCD. They also provide a safe place for bees to live. Win win!
€100 For €100 we can afford to conduct a scientific analysis of the honey from one of our sponsored apiaries. These scientific analyses help us identify which pesticides are particularly harmful to the wellbeing of healthy bee colonies.
€500 For €500 we can afford to set up a new hive at one of our existing apiaries, or lay the foundations for an entirely new apiary in a new location. Having access to honey from a variety of locations is vital to determining the causes of CCD.

Start a hive

If you or someone you know is interested in starting an apiary on your property, we can arrange to have the necessary materials sent to you! As long as you provide a safe environment for the bees to live, they will provide you with a constant supply of honey. All we ask is that you send us regular honey samples for scientific analysis. The rest of the honey is yours to keep! That's a pretty sweet deal.

What we do

BeeKeeper is an organisation that advocates for public awareness of Colony Collapse Disorder, and the preservation of the wild honeybee. Through the combined efforts of commercial apiarists, farmers, environmental scientists, and home beekeepers, we hope to mitigate the harmful effects that pesticides are having on wild honeybee colonies, and ultimately preserve one of the cornerstones of our natural ecosystem.
